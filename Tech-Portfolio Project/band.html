Band Saw

The purpose of the band saw is to CUT OUT SHAPES in wood/plastic

1) Eye protection must be worn while operating the Band Saw.

2) Complete attention must be directed toward workbeing done, NO TALKING.

3) Position hands on the material being cut so they are NOT IN FRONT of the blade.

4) Fingers should never be closer than 50 mm (2 inches) to the blade.

5) NEVER clear the table with your hands, use a piece bof scrap wood or a brush.

6) Lower the blade guard to the table after you have finished making the cuts and the blade has come to a complete stop.

7) Make all adjustments to the machine before you start the machine. Adjust the top blade guide approximately 6 mm (1/4 inch) above the material to be cut BEFORE starting the saw.

8) Cutting Round Stock can be dangerous on the Band Saw because it may spin when it contacts the blade. Use the special Doweling jig for this operation.

9) Make sure the blade has come to a FULL STOP before leaving the Band saw.

10) Occasionally the saw blade will break while the machine is in use. If this should occur, shut off the machine and notify the instructor immediately.

11) Never back out of a long or curved cut, turn the power off.

12) Use RELIEF CUTS when cutting sharp curves or irregular shapes

13) Make all cuts on the waste-side of the line

14) Do NOT stand to the right side of the band saw. If the blade breaks, it could hit you.
