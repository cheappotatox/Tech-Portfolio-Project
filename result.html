Exciting Moments:
Imagine the excitement as my dragster zipped down the track in a mere 1.589 seconds, reaching a fantastic speed of 12.5865 m/s or 45.31 km/h. It was faster than I ever imagined, even competing with the times of legendary athletes like Tyson Gay and Usain Bolt. This achievement highlights the power of creative design and careful engineering to make something truly awesome.
Engaging with mechanical engineering was like entering a new level in a game. I tinkered with various machines, applying clever design and physics tricks to elevate my dragster's performance. Tools like Computer-Aided Design (CAD) became my allies, making it easy to share and discuss ideas with my awesome team.
Fun with Tech and Website Creation:
Beyond the thrill of the race, creating this website was an adventure in itself. Learning the language of the web, almost like understanding the ABCs of the internet, opened up a world of possibilities. Using cool software like "Adobe Dreamweaver" felt like having a superpower. It made editing my site a breeze, emphasizing the importance of staying up-to-date with the latest in tech!
The web development journey taught me how to structure information and make it look cool. I played with colors, fonts, and layouts to create a space that truly represents my journey. It's amazing how tech tools can turn ideas into a digital reality for the world to see.
Opportunities for Improvement:
Beyond the cheers of success, there's always room for improvement. While my dragster performed admirably, a closer inspection reveals exciting opportunities for enhancement. 82g for a dragster of 8 inches long is beyond "heavy." My peers were hitting 50g with the same length. Therefore, shedding some weight would make my dragster faster. At the same time, maybe people noticed that air could get trapped under the CO2 dragster hole component. Therefore, refining the aerodynamics, and making subtle tweaks to the CO2 Dragster hole housing will give my dragster a superhero upgrade.
Lessons learned from the race also extend to unexpected places. Take the WD-40 situation, for example. Finding the right balance in everything, from design to race strategies, is the key to success.
Learning and Growing:
Every challenge brings lessons, and the dragster race was no exception. I discovered the joy of turning ideas into reality through hands-on experiences with machines and materials. Learning how to use technology to my advantage made the entire journey even more fulfilling.
The beauty of the dragster project was not just in the speed it achieved but in the skills and friendships built along the way. This project was more than a race; it was a lesson in teamwork and resilience.
Looking to the Future:
As I reflect on this incredible journey, I can't help but feel excited about what lies ahead. The world of design and engineering is vast, with endless possibilities waiting to be explored. Whether it's refining the dragster for another race or diving into new tech adventures, the journey is ongoing.
Looking forward, I see opportunities to delve deeper into coding, explore advanced design techniques, and maybe even create more websites. The dragster race was just the beginning, and with each experience, I'm gearing up for new challenges, new victories, and more opportunities to learn and grow.
